  • CAMA


    CAMA is healthcare design firm working with a client to identify how people with multiple diseases and HIV can be better served in the New Haven community. CAMA identified six major themes including Food and Nutrition which they wanted to research. Six Yale School of Management students were selected as Interview Lead to manage teams through the research process. I was selected as the Food and Nutrition Lead. I worked with a team of three to interview, immerse, and observe different pantries, social service groups, and restaurants to better understand how they support the people with multiple diseases and HIV.

    Manufacturing Client


    A large manufacturing client was looking to increase the accuracy of its Sales and Operations Planning (S&OP). The client wanted to include was planning for turnkey or completed components, which historically had never been projected. I developed a process and algorithm that utilized a variety of raw data to project various values to use to forecast. I also worked with various levels of the client ranging from VP’s of divisions to the technology teams in three global oilfield manufacturing plants to design the system. This project earned me my promotion for Analyst to Consultant.

    Junior Achievement


    After participating in the traditional Junior Achievement teaching in 2011, I recognized a need for the students to better understand business. I invented the Accenture Junior Achievement Request for Proposal Job Shadow. The program simulated the process of responding to a proposal for Blurb, a fake company, who has contacted Accenture to help them develop a social media website. The students broke up into groups of five and each student took on one of five role: Managing Director, Project Manager, Legal, Financial Analyst, and Design Architect. Each group was responsible for developing a presentation and presenting to an Accenture Managing Director. Over five years I led 7 session equipping 200 high school students with the skills to succeed and 420 volunteer hours toward the national corporate citizenship goal. The program earned me the 2014 Corporate Citizenship Award.

    Met


    My senior year of college I interned at the first undergraduate finance intern at the Metropolitan Museum of Art. Since the Met’s Development and Finance department’s system did not integrate with each other, the Met was looking to increase transparency between donations and investing. I reviewed the donation from 2009 and 2010 from the Development database and consolidated the financials. The consolidated database was utilized by the CFO and CIO to make cash flow projection and investment decisions, which was especially critical given the recession.

    Telecommunications Client


    I served at the counterpart to the Marketing Director of a midsize telecommunication in the midst of an acquisition. The acquisition would double the size of the company and the company wanted to be prepared to execute integration activities as soon as the Federal Communication Commission approved the deal. I worked with the Marketing Director to create a playbook, which outlined integration activities. The Playbook included resourcing, project plan, financials, and marketing campaign.

    IDEO


    IDEO New York hosted OpenIDEO Higher Education Workshop for the Yale School of Management Design and Innovation club. The workshop condensed IDEO’s process to a day. The first half of the afternoon was spent (re)searching and using engagement, observation, immersion, and analogies to understand the current state of financing higher education. The second half was devoted to ideation. My team focused on technology in higher education. Through (re)search we learned that online classes are cheaper but the lack of human connection in these classes discourage people from enrolling or completing courses. My design of an interactive virtual classroom integrated with a social media profile aimed to build connections with others enrolled in online courses. Details of the design can be found on the OpenIDEO platform titled “Online Class Personal Platform.”

    Yale School of Management: Charley's Place


    Charley’s Place is the café at the Yale School of Management. Charley’s Place is looking for opportunities to better connect with students. I am in the process of researching to develop an ideation session. Research has included interviews with students, staff and the chef, immersion through buying various meals, and observation of different times of the day. An ideation session is planned to be held January 2016.

    Jack S. Blanton Museum of Art


    My extracurricular focus at the University of Texas was serving as the President of the Blanton Student Guild, a student organization that managed the relationship between Jack S. Blanton Museum of Art and UT. My goal as the president was to solve the question, if early exposure to museum increase attendance and donations to museum, how should the Blanton draw in more student? I strategized opportunities for the museum to be more visible on campus though marketing and ambassadorship and and hosting 6 mixers totaling $45k and 5k attendees. I also represented the student body at the museum from everything from the director search committees to the museum certification.
